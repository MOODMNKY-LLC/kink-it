# Bonds System Overview

**Last Updated**: 2026-01-29

---

## What Are Bonds?

Bonds are relationship groups that connect members of your D/s dynamic. They replace the simple partner linking system with a more flexible, multi-member structure that supports various relationship types.

---

## Key Concepts

### Bond Types

- **Dyad**: Two-person relationship (traditional D/s pair)
- **Polycule**: Multi-person polyamorous network
- **Household**: Traditional leather family structure
- **Dynamic**: Flexible power exchange structure

### Bond Status

- **Forming**: Bond is being created, members joining
- **Active**: Bond is operational
- **Paused**: Bond is temporarily paused
- **Dissolved**: Bond has been dissolved

### Member Roles

- **Founder**: Created the bond, full permissions
- **Dominant**: Dominant role in the bond
- **Submissive**: Submissive role in the bond
- **Switch**: Can switch between roles
- **Member**: General bond member

---

## Benefits

1. **Multi-Member Support**: Connect multiple people in your dynamic
2. **Flexible Structures**: Support various relationship types
3. **Role-Based Features**: Features adapt to your role
4. **Privacy Control**: Private bonds with invite codes
5. **Permission System**: Granular control over bond management

---

## Use Cases

### Two-Person Dyad

Perfect for couples in a monogamous D/s relationship. Simple, focused, and private.

### Polycule

Ideal for polyamorous D/s relationships with multiple partners. Supports complex networks.

### Household

Suited for traditional leather families and long-term committed groups.

### Flexible Dynamic

Great for evolving relationships that don't fit traditional structures.

---

## Getting Started

1. **Create or Join**: During onboarding or later in settings
2. **Invite Members**: Share invite codes with partners
3. **Set Permissions**: Control who can invite and manage
4. **Start Using**: Access bond-specific features
