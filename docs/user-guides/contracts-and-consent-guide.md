## Introduction

The Contracts & Consent module helps you create, manage, and version your D/s covenant (contract). A covenant is a living document that formalizes your agreements, boundaries, and expectations. It serves as a communication tool and reference point for your dynamic.

---

## What Is a Covenant?

A **covenant** (also called a contract) is a written agreement between partners that outlines:
- Roles and responsibilities
- Rules and protocols
- Limits and boundaries
- Safewords and signals
- Communication expectations
- Review cadence

**Important**: A covenant is a tool for communication and structure, not a legal contract. It supports your dynamic but doesn't replace ongoing consent.

## Creating Your Covenant

### For Dominants

#### Step 1: Draft Initial Version

Start with the seed data example as a template:

1. **Copy the Example**: Use seed data as starting point
2. **Customize Sections**: Adapt to your dynamic
3. **Add Your Rules**: Reference rules you've created
4. **Include Boundaries**: Reference your boundaries list
5. **Set Review Date**: Choose review cadence (monthly, quarterly)

#### Step 2: Discuss with Partner

1. **Share Draft**: Let your submissive read it
2. **Answer Questions**: Clarify any confusion
3. **Negotiate Terms**: Adjust based on discussion
4. **Finalize Content**: Agree on final version

#### Step 3: Request Signatures

1. **Set Status**: Change to "Pending Signature"
2. **Sign Yourself**: Add your signature
3. **Request Partner's Signature**: They'll receive notification
4. **Activate**: Once both sign, set to "Active"

#### Step 4: Regular Reviews

1. **Schedule Reviews**: Set calendar reminders
2. **Discuss Changes**: What's working, what isn't
3. **Create New Version**: If changes needed
4. **Re-sign**: Both partners sign new version

### For Submissives

#### Understanding the Covenant

When your Dominant creates a covenant:

1. **Read Thoroughly**: Understand all sections
2. **Ask Questions**: Clarify anything unclear
3. **Negotiate**: Discuss terms you want to change
4. **Sign Consciously**: Only sign if you agree
5. **Request Reviews**: Ask for reviews if needed

#### Signing Process

1. **Review Draft**: Read the complete covenant
2. **Discuss Concerns**: Talk about any issues
3. **Request Changes**: If modifications needed
4. **Sign When Ready**: Only sign if you fully agree
5. **Keep Copy**: Save for your records

## Covenant Sections Explained

### 1. Introduction

**Purpose**: States why you're creating the covenant and who the parties are.

**Includes**:
- Purpose statement
- Participant names
- Effective date
- Review date

### 2. Roles & Responsibilities

**Purpose**: Defines what each partner agrees to do.

**Dominant Responsibilities**:
- Provide structure and leadership
- Ensure safety and wellbeing
- Make informed decisions
- Maintain communication

**Submissive Responsibilities**:
- Follow agreed rules
- Communicate openly
- Self-declare submission state
- Maintain transparency

### 3. Duration & Review

**Purpose**: Sets how long the covenant lasts and when to review.

**Options**:
- **Fixed Duration**: 6 months, 1 year, etc.
- **Indefinite**: Ongoing until terminated
- **Review Cadence**: Monthly, quarterly, annually

### 4. Rules & Protocols

**Purpose**: References your rules and protocols.

**Note**: Rules are managed separately but referenced here. The covenant explains the overall structure.

### 5. Limits & Boundaries

**Purpose**: Documents your hard and soft limits.

**Includes**:
- Hard limits (non-negotiable)
- Soft limits (discuss before)
- Reference to boundaries module

### 6. Safewords & Signals

**Purpose**: Establishes safety communication systems.

**Traffic Light System**:
- **Green**: Good, continue
- **Yellow**: Slow down, check in
- **Red**: Stop immediately
- **Blue**: Emotional need to stop

**Non-Verbal Signals**:
- Dropping object = Red
- Three taps = Yellow
- Snapping fingers = Red (if gagged)

### 7. Communication & Check-Ins

**Purpose**: Sets expectations for ongoing communication.

**Includes**:
- Daily check-in protocols
- Weekly review expectations
- How to request pauses
- Communication standards

### 8. Consent

**Purpose**: Acknowledges ongoing consent principles.

**Principles**:
- Ongoing and can be revoked
- Enthusiastic, not coerced
- Informed (understanding risks)
- Specific (not blanket)

### 9. Termination

**Purpose**: Explains how to end the covenant.

**Includes**:
- Either party can end
- No justification required
- Immediate pause of protocols
- Encouragement to discuss

## Digital Signatures

### How Signatures Work

1. **Dominant Creates**: Drafts covenant
2. **Sets to Pending**: Requests signatures
3. **Signs First**: Adds their signature
4. **Submissive Signs**: Adds their signature
5. **Becomes Active**: Status changes to "Active"

### Signature Status

- **Pending**: Waiting for signature
- **Signed**: Signature added
- **Declined**: Signature refused (rare)

## Safewords & Signals

### Traffic Light System

**Green**: Everything is good, continue or intensify
- Use when: Feeling good, want more
- Response: Continue as planned

**Yellow**: Slow down, check in, approaching limit
- Use when: Getting intense, need adjustment
- Response: Ease up, check in, adjust

**Red**: Stop immediately, scene ends
- Use when: Need to stop completely
- Response: Stop all activity, check in, aftercare

**Blue**: Emotional need to stop (non-physical)
- Use when: Emotionally overwhelmed
- Response: Stop, emotional support, aftercare

### Non-Verbal Signals

For situations where you can't speak:
- **Dropping Object**: Red (stop immediately)
- **Three Taps**: Yellow (slow down)
- **Snapping Fingers**: Red (if gagged)

### Important Notes

- Safeword use is NEVER punished
- Always leads to immediate check-in
- Can be used at any time
- No questions asked when used

## Consent Tracking

### Ongoing Consent

Consent is:
- **Ongoing**: Can be withdrawn at any time
- **Enthusiastic**: Not coerced or pressured
- **Informed**: Understanding risks and benefits
- **Specific**: Not blanket consent

### Consent Principles

1. **Can Be Withdrawn**: At any time, for any reason
2. **Must Be Enthusiastic**: Not just "okay"
3. **Requires Understanding**: Know what you're agreeing to
4. **Is Specific**: Not general permission

### Consent Log

The app tracks:
- When consent was given
- What was consented to
- Any withdrawals
- Consent history

## Best Practices

### For Dominants

1. **Start Simple**: First covenant doesn't need to be perfect
2. **Discuss First**: Don't surprise with a covenant
3. **Be Flexible**: Willing to negotiate terms
4. **Review Regularly**: Check what's working
5. **Respect Limits**: Never pressure about boundaries

### For Submissives

1. **Read Carefully**: Understand what you're agreeing to
2. **Ask Questions**: Clarify anything unclear
3. **Negotiate**: Don't sign if uncomfortable
4. **Request Reviews**: Ask for reviews if needed
5. **Know Your Rights**: You can withdraw consent anytime

### Covenant Design

1. **Clear Language**: Easy to understand
2. **Specific Terms**: Avoid vague language
3. **Realistic Expectations**: Achievable agreements
4. **Flexible Structure**: Can evolve over time
5. **Regular Reviews**: Don't let it get stale

## Troubleshooting

### "Covenant Feels Too Formal"

**Problem**: Covenant feels like a legal document rather than a relationship tool.

**Solution**:
- Use personal language
- Focus on relationship, not legality
- Keep it conversational
- Remember it's a tool, not a contract

### "Partner Won't Sign"

**Problem**: Submissive hesitant to sign covenant.

**Solution**:
- Discuss concerns openly
- Address specific worries
- Modify terms if needed
- Don't pressure - consent is key

### "Need to Change Terms"

**Problem**: Covenant needs updates but already signed.

**Solution**:
- Create new version
- Discuss changes together
- Both sign new version
- Archive old version
