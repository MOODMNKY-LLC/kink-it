## Daily Check-Ins

### The Traffic Light System

**Green**: Good, focused, ready
- Feeling energized and capable
- Ready for tasks and protocols
- No major concerns

**Yellow**: Some concerns, need support
- Feeling overwhelmed or stressed
- Might need lighter expectations
- Need to discuss something

**Red**: Distressed, overwhelmed, need immediate attention
- Significant emotional distress
- Unable to function normally
- Need immediate support

**Blue**: Emotional need to stop (non-physical)
- Emotionally overwhelmed
- Need to pause activities
- Requires emotional support

### How to Check In

1. **Select Your Status**: Green, Yellow, Red, or Blue
2. **Add Notes**: Explain your state briefly
3. **Submit**: Your partner receives notification
4. **Follow Up**: Partner responds appropriately

### For Submissives

**When to Check In**:
- Daily (morning or evening)
- When status changes significantly
- Before important activities
- After challenging experiences

**What to Include**:
- Your emotional state
- Energy level
- Any concerns or needs
- What's working well

### For Dominants

**How to Respond**:
- **Green**: Acknowledge, continue as planned
- **Yellow**: Check in, offer support, adjust expectations
- **Red**: Immediate attention, pause activities, provide support
- **Blue**: Emotional support, pause play, aftercare

## Messages

### Message Types

**Praise Messages**: Recognition and acknowledgment
- "I noticed how well you completed..."
- "Your attention to detail was..."
- "Well done, [honorific]"

**Correction Messages**: Guidance and adjustment
- "I noticed [behavior], let's adjust..."
- "For next time, remember to..."
- "Here's what I'd like to see..."

**Alignment Messages**: Checking in about protocols
- "How are you feeling about [protocol]?"
- "Is [rule] working for you?"
- "Should we adjust [expectation]?"

**Repair Messages**: Addressing issues
- "I noticed [concern], let's talk..."
- "How can I better support you?"
- "What do you need from me?"

### Sending Messages

1. **Choose Recipient**: Select your partner
2. **Write Message**: Clear, specific communication
3. **Send**: Partner receives notification
4. **Track**: View message history

## Scene Debriefs

### What Is a Scene Debrief?

A scene debrief is a structured reflection after a BDSM scene that helps:
- Process the experience
- Identify what worked
- Plan for future scenes
- Ensure aftercare needs are met

### Debrief Structure

**What Went Well**:
- Activities that felt good
- Moments of connection
- Successful elements

**What to Adjust**:
- Things to change next time
- Intensity adjustments
- Timing modifications

**Emotional State**:
- How you felt during scene
- How you feel now
- Any drop or aftercare needs

**Aftercare**:
- What aftercare was received
- What additional aftercare is needed
- How you're feeling now

**Safewords Used**:
- Did you use safewords?
- If yes, what happened?
- How was it handled?

**Overall Rating**:
- 1-5 scale rating
- Would you repeat?
- Notes and thoughts

### Creating a Debrief

1. **After Scene**: Wait until you're ready
2. **Fill Out Form**: Complete debrief structure
3. **Be Honest**: Accurate feedback helps
4. **Submit**: Share with your partner
5. **Discuss**: Talk about it together

## Best Practices

### For Both Partners

1. **Check In Daily**: Regular communication maintains connection
2. **Be Honest**: Accurate status helps adjust expectations
3. **Use Prompts**: Structured questions deepen understanding
4. **Debrief Scenes**: Always process after intense scenes
5. **Communicate Needs**: Don't wait for problems to escalate

### For Dominants

1. **Respond Promptly**: Acknowledge check-ins quickly
2. **Adjust Expectations**: Based on Yellow/Red status
3. **Use Prompts Regularly**: Deepen connection
4. **Always Debrief**: Process scenes together
5. **Listen Actively**: Hear what your submissive needs

### For Submissives

1. **Check In Honestly**: Accurate status protects you
2. **Use Safewords**: Don't hesitate if needed
3. **Communicate Needs**: Ask for what you need
4. **Debrief Thoroughly**: Help improve future scenes
5. **Express Gratitude**: Acknowledge good leadership

## Troubleshooting

### "Check-Ins Feel Like a Chore"

**Problem**: Daily check-ins feel burdensome.

**Solution**:
- Simplify check-in format
- Reduce frequency if needed
- Make it meaningful, not robotic
- Focus on quality over quantity

### "Partner Doesn't Respond to Check-Ins"

**Problem**: Dominant doesn't acknowledge check-ins.

**Solution**:
- Discuss importance of acknowledgment
- Set expectations for response time
- Use prompts to start conversations
- Address in weekly review

### "Don't Know What to Say in Debriefs"

**Problem**: Struggling to articulate scene experience.

**Solution**:
- Use debrief prompts as guide
- Start with simple questions
- Focus on feelings, not just facts
- Practice makes it easier
